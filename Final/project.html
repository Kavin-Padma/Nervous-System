The Nervous System

What is the Nervous System?

The nervous system is the system in charge of sending messages throughout the body. It is made up of the brain, spinal cord and nerves. There are two parts to the nervous system. They are the central nervous system and the peripheral nervous system. The central nervous system is made up of the brain and spinal cord and is what interprets the information, and makes decisions based on the messages that are relayed from the peripheral nervous system. The peripheral nervous system is incharge of sending infomation to and from the brain to the rest of the body. It is made up of all the nerves in the body.

The Dissection Lab

Overview

This video accurately describes how to dissect a frog. It will demonstrate all the proper techniques needed to dissect the organism and will also explain the anatomy of a frog in detail. The dissection is carried out by Anish Padma and Arushi Sarkar. The video recorder is Jeeya Parekh and the other two helpers are Jia Shibi and Kavin Padma.

Procedure

  1. Gather the Supplies - For this frog dissection, the will need a dissection tray, scissors, tweezers, and pins to hold the specimen in place. Remember to wear safety goggles and gloves when in the vicinity of the specimen. Also, always remember to cut away from you, and to treat the specimen as humanly as possible.
  2. Prepping for Dissection - First, cut open the bag holding the specimen and place it on the dissection tray. Any preserving fluids found in the bags can be properly disposed of in the sink.
  3. Examining the External Features and Frog's Mouth - Examine the external features of the frog. For examining the mouth of the frog, pull the jaw open and make small incisions in the corners of the jaw to open the mouth wider for observation.
  4. Cutting the Frog and Pinning the Frog - Flip the frog over, onto its back. Using the tweezers, pinch the loose skin at the center of the frog’s stomach and make a small cut using the sharp side of the scissors. Then insert the scissors into the cut, and using the curved side of the scissors, cut along the vertical midline from the mouth to the groin. Then make two cuts lateral to the previous cut on each side, lift the skin flaps that have now been made, and expose the abdominal muscles. Then, make another cut into the abdominal muscles, and again using the curved side of the scissors, cut along the vertical midline to the mouth, and then make 2 cuts lateral to the previous cut on each side. This now exposes the organs of the specimen. Now pin down the muscle and skin flaps down to make it easier to examine the organs without the specimen moving around.
  5. Examining the Internal Organs of the Frog - After examining the frog’s organs, unpin the muscle and skin flaps from the trap and wrap them back around the frog’s organs.
  6. Cutting the Head of the Frog and Examining its Brain - Then using one of the pins, pin both sides of the flaps horizontally to each other to ensure that the organs will not spill out. Now flip the frog back over, onto its stomach. Now, near the neck of the frog, start making a vertical incision that ends at the frog's nostrils. Now open the skin flaps and pin them down. This will now expose brain, and you can now examine the brain.

Neurons Diagram

The diagram of the neuron, shown to the left, depicts all of the key organelles of a neuron. Two of the key structures of the neuron are the dendrites and axons. Dendrites receive messages from other neurons while axons send messages to other neurons.

Hydraulic Hand

The 3-D model of the hand accurately depicts how electric signals move from the brain to a part of the body, in this case a hand. The tube is the nerve and the water within the tubes are the electromagnetic signals.

Brain Diagram

The 3-D model of the brain describes and depicts all parts of a brain. The different colors depict the different sections of the brain. A description of the brain is found next to 3-D diagram of the brain.

Alzheimer's Brain Diagram

The diagram of the Alzheimer's brain shows all of the structures of the spinal cord including things like the cervical, thoracic, and lumbar spinal cords. The diagram will also depict the surrounding structures of the spinal cord like vertebrae.
